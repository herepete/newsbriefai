In brief — Ubisoft has temporarily shut down the servers for Rainbow Six Siege following a significant hack that compromised player accounts and in-game currency.

Why it matters

  • The breach allowed hackers to manipulate in-game systems, including granting players billions in virtual currency.
  • Ubisoft's response includes shutting down the game's marketplace and servers to address the security issue.
  • The incident highlights the vulnerabilities in online gaming platforms and the potential financial implications for companies.

Explainer

Ubisoft's popular game, Rainbow Six Siege, faced a severe security breach that led to the shutdown of its servers and marketplace. Hackers gained unauthorized access to the game's systems, allowing them to issue bans, unlock all in-game items, and distribute a staggering 2 billion R6 Credits to players. This virtual currency has a real-world value of approximately $13.33 million, based on Ubisoft's pricing structure. The company has taken immediate action to contain the situation by shutting down all servers across PC and consoles while they work to resolve the breach. Such incidents underscore the ongoing challenges that gaming companies face in securing their platforms against cyber threats. The outcome of this situation could impact player trust and Ubisoft's reputation in the long run.
