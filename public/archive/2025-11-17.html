Today’s 2-Minute UK AI Brief

17 November 2025

TL;DR — Paul McCartney has released a silent track as part of a protest against AI's impact on the music industry in the UK.

Why it matters

  • The protest highlights growing concerns among UK artists about copyright infringement by AI technologies.
  • McCartney's actions may influence UK policymakers to consider stricter regulations on AI-generated content.
  • The rise of AI-generated music, such as the US band Breaking Rust topping charts, raises questions about the future of the UK music industry.

Explainer

Paul McCartney, the former Beatle, has joined a group of artists in protesting against artificial intelligence's role in music creation by releasing a nearly silent track titled "Is This What We Want." This initiative underscores significant concerns within the UK music community regarding copyright violations and the potential for AI to undermine the artistic integrity of human musicians. As AI-generated music gains traction—evidenced by the AI band Breaking Rust reaching the top of the Billboard Country chart in the US—UK artists are increasingly vocal about the need for protections against what they perceive as threats to their livelihoods. The UK government and regulatory bodies may need to respond to these concerns, potentially leading to new policies that address the intersection of AI technology and copyright law. The outcome of this protest could shape the future landscape of the UK music industry, prompting discussions on how to balance innovation with the rights of artists.
