Today’s 2-Minute UK AI Brief

16 December 2025

TL;DR — The UK government has launched a taskforce aimed at increasing female participation in the technology sector to help close the gender gap in AI.

Why it matters

  • The initiative responds to calls for action to meet the UK's ambitious AI development goals.
  • Increasing women's representation in tech is seen as crucial for innovation and economic growth.
  • The taskforce aims to create pathways for women to enter and lead in the tech industry.

Explainer

The UK government has recently established a Women in Tech taskforce, spearheaded by Technology Secretary Liz Kendall, to address the significant gender gap in the technology sector. This move comes in the wake of recommendations that emphasize the need for a more inclusive workforce to achieve the UK's ambitious objectives in artificial intelligence (AI) development. The taskforce's goals include not only increasing the number of women entering tech roles but also ensuring they have opportunities to advance into leadership positions. This initiative is particularly important as the UK aims to bolster its position in the global AI landscape, where diverse teams are often linked to greater innovation and problem-solving capabilities. By fostering a more inclusive environment, the government hopes to enhance the overall performance and competitiveness of the UK tech sector.
